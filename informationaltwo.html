Informational Two

Expanding brackets

When encountering algebra studying the question and what answer you are trying to figure out is key, if you find the standard algebraic formule to be confusing on a piece of paper write it down in standard notation without effecting the equation, for example with double brackets the equation
(y + 2)(y + 3) simply put is (y + 2) x (y + 3).

Factorisation

Factorisation or factorising is more or less the opposite of expanding brackets by finding the Highest Common Denominator or HCD, HCD is a number that goes into all variables a set number of times or simply put you can divide all numbers by that in the equation/expression and then place it outside the brackets, for example 3x + 6x then becomes 3(x + 2x).
