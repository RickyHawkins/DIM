Brief Overview

What is algebra?

Explanation

Algebra is the application of variables into mathematics, these variables are often denoted as italic subscripted letter's within the equation or expression, as stated before these are variables meaning that the number could be anything as it is currently unknown, this leaves us with the problem to figure it out using some handy tricks and backwards calculation to figure it out.

Equation or Expression?

The difference between an equation and an expression is quite simple, an expression doesn't have an equals sign and is usually denoted for example as "2x + 5" and an equasion can be denoted as "2x + 5 = 15", these can be expanded on to even become quadratic eqations but they will be dealt with at a later time in your syllabus.
